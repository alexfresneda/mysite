2020 was, to say the least, a weird year. After graduating in summer and starting work, I found myself with a lot of free time in the evenings. However, life had changed quite a bit and so I wasn't able to spend my free time they way I used to - team sports and socialising wasn't really possible. So towards the end of 2020, I took the challenge of spending 100 days learning SwiftUI. I really enjoyed this challenge, which eventually took me to building my own iOS apps and starting Basico.



But one of the things I enjoyed the most was the challenge of spending around 1 hour per day on learning something new. So in mid-January 2021 I started a new challenge: 100 days of Daily UI. This challenge, created by Daily UI, consists of spending 100 days designing interfaces. Each weekday, I receive an email with a design challenge. I usually spend between 30 minutes and 1 hour of my free time completing the challenges on Figma, and posting the final designs on dribbble.
