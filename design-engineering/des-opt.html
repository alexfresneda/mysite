Design Optimisation

This project consisted in the design and optimisation of a micro wind turbine. Combining aerodynamics principles, data analysis and simulations in MATLAB, a set of blades was designed in Autodesk Inventor, built and tested to optimise for maximum power output.



The Challenge

For the Design Optimisation course at The University of Bath, I was challenged to design the optimum set of blades for a micro wind turbine. The challenge included various constraints such as a maximum rotor diameter of 1 meter. The final design had to be tested in the University Labs and the performance would be evaluated in terms of Voltage, Current and Rotor Speed. 



The Process

Initially, aerodynamic theory research was carried out to familiarise with the theory behind the operation of wind turbines. After this, a full-factorial experiment was conducted using design of experiments, followed by analysis of variance (ANOVA) to determine which factors had a significant impact on the output of a wind turbine. Based on the theoretical research and the experimental results, a suitable aerofoil profile was selected to optimise performance for the given testing scenario. Following this, MATLAB was used to obtain the optimum dimensions of the blade using Blade Element Momentum Theory.

The results from the MATLAB simulations guided the design of the first set of blades. In order to validate the results from the simulations, a model of the rotor was built and tested. After testing the initial set, improvements were carried out to increase the speed of rotation. This was done by analysing the data collected during the experiments and modifying the simulations accordingly to approximate the MATLAB model to reality.



The Result

The final set of blades, which was built combining a laser-cut MDF structure with 3D printed material and wrapped with lightweight tape (left rotor on the left picture), achieved a maximum rotational speed of 410 rpm. It achieved the highest speed of the course, which was considered a success. Upon reflection, it was clear that the selection of a thinner aerofoil and lightweight materials had a significant impact on performance.
